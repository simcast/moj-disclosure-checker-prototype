2. Telling people about your cautions or convictions

If a basic criminal record check is requested

When a caution or conviction is spent you do not need to tell people about it, or the offence that led to it, if they’re carrying out a basic criminal record check.

If a standard or enhanced criminal record check is requested

You will usually need to tell people about spent cautions or convictions if they’re carrying out a standard or enhanced criminal record check.

3. How to use this information

As you’ve not been asked for specific details about your caution or conviction, this service can only give you general guidance about when it becomes spent.

The result you’ve been given might be wrong if you:

  • entered any incorrect information, such as approximate dates
  • did not serve your sentence in full as the court ordered you to, or
  • did not stick to the conditions of your sentence
  • were released on bail at any time
Some spent cautions and convictions are ’protected’ or ‘filtered’ and will not show up on a standard or enhanced criminal record check.

If you plan to use the information this service has given you to decide whether or not to disclose a caution or conviction on your criminal record, it’s recommended that you first get specific professional or legal advice.
