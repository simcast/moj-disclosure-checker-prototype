What this result means

Based on what you’ve told us, this conviction and any rehabilitation period is now spent.

If you entered any approximate dates, the result you’ve been given will be incorrect.

If a basic criminal record check is requested

When a caution or conviction is spent you do not need to tell people about it, or the offence that led to it, if they’re requesting a basic criminal record check.

If a standard or enhanced criminal record check is requested

You will need to tell people about spent cautions or convictions if they’re requesting a standard or enhanced criminal record check.

Some spent cautions and convictions are ’protected’ or ‘filtered’ and will not show up on a standard or enhanced criminal record check.

If you have more than one caution or conviction

The result you've been given might be incorrect if you have other convictions.

You should get professional or legal advice if you:

  • got another conviction during a rehabilitation period
  • are applying for certain sensitive jobs, such as a policeman or lawyer
