Terms, conditions and privacy

The check when a caution or conviction expires service is provided by the Ministry of Justice (MoJ).

Its purpose is to help users work out whether or not they need to tell organisations (such as employers, places of education or insurance companies) about a caution or conviction on their criminal record.

The service tells users when a single caution or conviction expires (becomes ‘spent’) according to the current law in England and Wales.

What the service does

The service asks users for:

  • the type of conviction they were given
  • the date they were convicted
  • their age when convicted

This information is checked against the current law and users are given the date the conviction expires.

Some organisations will still need to know about cautions and convictions that have expired, depending on the application that’s being made.

Our responsibilities

The expiry date given at the end of the service is based solely on the information provided by the service user.

If any of the information a user inputs is incorrect, the date given at the end might also be incorrect.

MoJ is not responsible for:

  • people disclosing their expired cautions or convictions when they weren’t required to
  • how and when employers ask for information about people’s criminal records

Privacy

This service is completely anonymous to use. Users are not asked for any personal information that could be used to identify them (such as their name, address or prisoner number).

If you have more than one caution or conviction on your criminal record, you’ll need to work out the expiry date of each one separately.
