Check when a caution or conviction expires

Use this service to check if you need to tell people (such as employers or colleges) about a caution or conviction.

Most cautions and convictions expire (become 'spent') after a certain length of time.

You do not normally need to tell anyone about them after they expire, but some jobs need a full criminal background check.

You will not be asked for any personal information (such as your name or address) when using the service.
Start now

Before you start

To use this service you need to:

  • live in England or Wales
  • know the type of caution or conviction you were given
  • know the date you were cautioned or convicted

If you don’t have this information you can read the guidance on when cautions and convictions become spent.

If you have more than one caution or conviction on your criminal record, you’ll need to work out the expiry date of each one separately.
